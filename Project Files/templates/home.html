🌍 Human Development Index (HDI)

Understanding the Human Development Index

The Human Development Index (HDI) is a composite statistic that measures a country's overall development based on three important dimensions:

  • Life Expectancy at Birth: Measures the average lifespan and reflects health and healthcare quality
  • Education: Includes both Expected Years of Schooling and Mean Years of Schooling, indicating access and quality of education
  • Gross National Income (GNI) Per Capita: Reflects the standard of living and economic productivity

HDI Categories

Countries are classified into four categories based on their HDI score:

  • Low HDI (0.3 - 0.4): Countries with developing infrastructure and limited resources
  • Medium HDI (0.4 - 0.7): Countries showing moderate development with improving conditions
  • High HDI (0.7 - 0.8): Developed nations with strong infrastructure and services
  • Very High HDI (0.8 - 1.0): Highly developed nations with excellent living standards

About This Project

This intelligent application uses a Machine Learning Linear Regression model to predict the Human Development Index score based on user-provided values for life expectancy, education metrics, and income. The model analyzes the relationships between these key development indicators and provides both a numerical HDI score and the corresponding development category.
